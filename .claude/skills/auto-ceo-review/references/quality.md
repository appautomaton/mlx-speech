# CEO Review Quality

Load this reference only before appending the product review to `SPEC.md`.

## Product Review Anti-Patterns

- Strategy filler: broad market or leverage language that does not affect the verdict.
- Unsupported value claims: "high impact" without user, behavior, or business evidence.
- Rubber-stamp approval: approving without naming the strongest risk.
- Vague differentiation: "unique" or "better" without saying compared to what.
- Scope inflation: expanding the bet beyond the framed change.

## Better Shape

- Restate the bet with a specific user, action, reason, and risk.
- Separate what the evidence supports from what remains assumed.
- Use `approved_with_risks` when the direction is sound but important uncertainty remains.
- Return to framing when the value claim cannot be evaluated.

## Prose Hygiene

Product reviews attract strategic filler and rubber-stamp language. The review should make a call, not sound impressive.

Scan for:
- "strategically positioned", "well-positioned to capture": name the specific bet
- "aligns with broader trends": name which trend and why it helps this change
- "significant market opportunity": name the user, the action, and the evidence
- Persuasive authority tropes ("at its core", "the real question is")
- Generic positive conclusions that avoid the strongest risk

Before: "This initiative is strategically well-positioned to capture a significant market opportunity, aligning with broader industry trends toward developer productivity."
After: "Bet: data analysts at mid-market SaaS will pay $50/mo for automated anomaly detection because they spend 4hr/week writing manual SQL queries. Risk: they may not trust automated alerts enough to stop checking manually."

## Final Check

If the review does not change whether planning should proceed, revise it.
